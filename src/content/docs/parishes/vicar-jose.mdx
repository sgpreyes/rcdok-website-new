### San Ildefonso Parish

<ParishProfile

  address="Estrella corner L. R. Yangco Sts., Navotas City"

>
It is dedicated to Saint Ildefonso, an archbishop from Toledo in the 7th century, who was well-known for his devotion to the Virgin Mary. The parish’s rich history reflects the local community’s cultural legacy. It regularly hosts religious events that involve parishioners of all ages, such as masses and novenas. Traditional Filipino and Spanish colonial elements are combined in the church’s architecture. It acts as a hub for social outreach initiatives, catechism, and spiritual development. To promote communal cohesion, the parish actively participates in regional festivals and celebrations. Its continuous initiatives aim to enhance its members’ moral principles, faith, and altruism.
</ParishProfile>

### San Lorenzo Ruiz and Companion Martyrs Parish

<ParishProfile 
  name="San Lorenzo Ruiz and Companion Martyrs Parish" 
  img={sanloreImg} 
  fbLink="https://www.facebook.com/slrcmp/" 
  priest="Rev. Fr. Allan Lopez, OP"
  address="Phase 1-C, Kaunlaran Village, North Bay, Boulevard South, Navotas City"
  contact="Tel. 8-351-0886"
>
Lorenzo Ruiz, the first Filipino saint, and his colleague martyrs are honored at San Lorenzo Ruiz and Companion Martyrs Parish. The church serves a growing Catholic community and is situated in a vibrant urban area. For parishioners, it offers regular masses, confessions, catechism lessons, and spiritual retreats. The church structure honors traditional Catholic themes while reflecting modern Filipino style. The church actively supports health initiatives, education, and the underprivileged through community service. Special masses, processions, and neighborhood events are held on the feast day of San Lorenzo Ruiz. Inspired by the lives of its patron saint, the parish places a strong emphasis on bravery, faith, and resiliency. For the local faithful, it acts as a hub for moral instruction and spiritual development.
</ParishProfile>
